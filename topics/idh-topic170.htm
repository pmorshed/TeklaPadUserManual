3- Syntax improvement:

While using OfType<T> method for "Type" filtering, you can simply skip the source (Database/DrawingDatabase) in your query/command to get all Tekla objects with specific type. Example:

 

filters the "Part" objects from Database (selected objects):

Database.OfType<Part>()

filters all "Part" objects from model:

OfType<Part>()

 

 

Learn more here

 

 

4- New xAPI features. Including:

 

•IsLike: Simple & handy pattern matching that also supports wildcards (e.g. * , ? ).

Examples:

will find any text prefixed with b/

{Target}.IsLike("b/*")

 

will find everything with 2 characters in front of a ’4’ like w14, w24, HF4

{Target}.IsLike("??4")

 

will find texts 0-9 prefixed with b/

{Target}.IsLike("b/#")

 

You can also choose more than one pattern:

will find texts 1,2,3 prefixed with b/

{Target}.IsLike("b/1, b/2, b/3)

 

 

will find texts 1-4 prefixed with b/ or c/

{Target}.IsLike("[bc]/1, [bc]/2, [bc]/3, [bc]/4")

 

will find texts 1-3 prefixed with b/ thru e/

{Target}.IsLike("[b-e]/1, [b-e]/2, [b-e]/3")

 

 

•GetTeklaUDA: Retrieves user defined attribute text from model objects. The most advantage is that you can use the "Smart Input" parameter. With this option (which is enabled by default) you don't have to worry about uppercase and lowercase letters.
